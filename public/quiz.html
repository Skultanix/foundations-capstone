Scenario One: Friend’s Face

Scenario:

You find yourself at a party with a close friend of yours. It’s one of those parties where a friend of a friend invites their friends and by the time the event happens almost nobody knows the host and feels like they’re surrounded by strangers. But hey, there’s a nice spread (including a chocolate fountain), the music isn’t too bad, and let’s be real, you had nothing better to do on this particular Friday night.

While you’re chatting with your friend you notice that they seemed to have splashed a small but noticeable amount of chocolate fondue on their forehead and they haven’t seemed to notice. What will you do?

Scenario Four: OnlyFlans

Scenario:

You find yourself at the head of a mid-size software engineering company. Your most recent client surprisingly is world famous baker Saul Bollywood. Together you’ve been working on an app called OnlyFlans wherein users can make online profiles and share recipes, videos, and images and monetize their content how they see fit.

The app is new, and the team hasn’t really flushed out the terms of service. Unfortunately, some enterprising Twitch streamers have caught wind of this app and have started putting out content that’s—well—far spicier than any flan should be. This has led to an explosion in new users and subsequently, revenue. Mr. Bollywood for his part is not terribly thrilled about the new content as he was hoping to have something that he’d, “be able to show my nan without blushing”. The two of you are meeting in person tomorrow. What will you do?

Scenario Six: The Genie

Scenario:

You’re walking along a beach and look down to see a lamp—the sort used in the middle east during the Middle Ages. Perhaps it’s the intricate design on the lamp or the fact that this looks like a missing prop from an Aladdin play, but it catches your eye, and you pick it up. You notice a smudge on the surface of the lamp and rub it.

Poof! A man that looks like a member of the Blue Man Group and sounds suspiciously like Robin Williams appears in a burst of blue smoke. The man introduces himself as a genie and offers to grant you a single wish. You protest and highlight that granting three wishes is the norm, to which the genie cryptically responds, “not in this economy”. What will you wish for?
